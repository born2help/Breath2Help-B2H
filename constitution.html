B2H Constitution

Principles, Governance & Ethical Foundations

← Back Home Transparency Tokenomics

Purpose of the Constitution

The Constitution of B2H Global Foundation defines the ethical, operational, and governance principles that ensure all activities serve humanity first. It establishes accountability, transparency, and sustainability as the foundation of every action.

Core Principles

  • Humanitarian-first participation with ethical responsibility
  • Transparency and accountability in all actions
  • No personal gain for founders, trustees, or contributors
  • Faith-aligned financial practices (no Riba / interest)
  • Long-term sustainability and mission continuity

Governance Structure

B2H operates under structured governance including ethical councils and operational committees. Founder stewardship exists only to safeguard the mission. No single individual can override accountability, ethical compliance, or humanitarian purpose.

Founder Stewardship

The Founder maintains ultimate oversight for ethical guidance and mission protection but holds no financial ownership. Authority is exercised solely to prevent misuse, uphold ethical compliance, and guide humanitarian allocations.

Participant Responsibilities

  • Understand the purpose and limitations of B2H
  • Engage voluntarily and with humanitarian intent
  • Respect ethical guidelines and community trust
  • Perform independent verification or due diligence if necessary

Risk Management

Participation in B2H carries inherent risks. There are no guarantees of financial returns, asset value increase, or liquidity. Risk is mitigated through ethical governance, mission-aligned structures, and operational transparency within safe boundaries.

Final Ethical Commitment

B2H pledges perpetual ethical stewardship. Every action, burn, and allocation is designed to serve humanity, protect dignity, and maintain trust — ensuring a lasting positive impact.
