B2H Constitution

Principles, Governance & Ethical Foundations

← Back Home Transparency Tokenomics

Purpose of the Constitution

The Constitution of B2H Global Foundation defines the principles, structures, and rules that govern all activities. It ensures the project remains humanitarian-first, ethical, and sustainable for the long term.

Core Principles

  • Humanity-first participation and ethical responsibility
  • Transparency and accountability in all operations
  • No personal enrichment for founders, trustees, or contributors
  • Faith-aligned financial practices (no Riba / interest)
  • Long-term sustainability and mission continuity

Governance Structure

B2H is governed by ethical councils, operational committees, and founder stewardship rules. No single entity or individual has unchecked authority. Decision-making is structured to protect humanitarian outcomes and ensure continuity.

Founder Stewardship

The founder maintains ultimate oversight for safeguarding the mission but holds no financial ownership. Authority is exercised to prevent misuse, uphold ethical compliance, and guide humanitarian allocations.

Participant Responsibilities

  • Understand the purpose and limitations of B2H
  • Engage responsibly and with humanitarian intent
  • Respect ethical guidelines and community trust
  • Perform independent verification if needed

Risk Management

B2H participation involves risk. There are no guarantees of financial returns, price appreciation, or market liquidity. Risks are mitigated through ethical governance, mission-aligned structures, and transparency within operational boundaries.

Final Ethical Commitment

B2H commits to perpetual ethical stewardship, ensuring that every action, burn, and allocation serves humanity, protects dignity, and reinforces trust.
