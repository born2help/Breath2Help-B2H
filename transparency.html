Transparency Framework

Trust through responsibility — not exposure

At B2H, transparency exists to build trust, not to expose vulnerabilities or create risk. Every action is guided by ethics, compassion, and responsibility.

Why Transparency Has Limits

Absolute exposure can endanger those we seek to help. Ethical transparency ensures safety while maintaining accountability.

Responsible disclosure protects people — not power.

What B2H Discloses

  • Core mission and ethical constraints
  • Governance framework & safeguards
  • Participation principles and risk guidance
  • Humanitarian impact summaries (high-level)
  • Use-of-funds categories (non-exploitable)

What B2H Keeps Private

  • Wallet addresses & internal accounts
  • Logistics of aid delivery
  • Beneficiary identities
  • Security-sensitive operational data
  • Private continuity designations

Protection of people comes before total visibility.

Ethical Financial Transparency

B2H does not provide speculative projections or price targets. Transparency is guided by ethics, not by curiosity or hype.

Use of Funds — Responsible Disclosure

  • Humanitarian support & relief
  • Operational continuity
  • Security, compliance, & ethical oversight
  • Logistics & coordination (minimal)

Luxury, excess, or profit extraction is strictly forbidden.

Privacy Protects Dignity

Publicizing suffering can create stigma and exploitation. B2H prioritizes dignity over exposure.

Independent Review (Safe & Private)

Trusted ethical reviewers may verify processes privately. They hold no control, authority, or influence over B2H operations.

Handling Misinformation

Any claim of guaranteed returns or special authority does not represent B2H.

Silence is intentional where hype risks harm.

Amanah — Sacred Trust

In Islamic accountability, amanah is sacred. Careless truth that harms trust is as damaging as a lie.

Final Principle

B2H always chooses responsible restraint over harmful exposure.

Transparency serves people, not the other way around.
